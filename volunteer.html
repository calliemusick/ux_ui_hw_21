As a team, we work to connect vulnerable individuals who are experiencing homelessness with valuable medical and mental health services.


We help patients develop a medical care plan and navigate the healthcare system by assisting with appointment scheduling, providing transportation, accompanying patients to local medical services and advocating for patients during their appointments.

OPEN POSITIONS FOR:

Volunteers with medical, mental health or social work backgrounds:

We're currently accepting applications for:

  • COMMUNITY HEALTH WORKER / OUTREACH
  • MEDICAL STAFF
  • MENTAL HEALTH CLINICIAN

As a medically-trained volunteer traveling alongside our patients on their journey towards health, you will work with many different professionals and gain valuable insight into health care. Perhaps more importantly, you will grow to understand the obstacles faced by marginalized populations and be able to make a difference in an individual’s life.


Instead of being an observer to the repercussions of healthcare inequity, you can actively contribute to a solution!

Community Health Worker (CHW) / Outreach

Duties include, but are not limited to:

  • Gathering all documentation in the patient’s chart during an outreach encounter
  • Taking vitals and assisting the medical team
  • Being present during the medical encounter

This opportunity is a great fit for students interested in medicine, social services and mental health.

Medical Staff

Duties include, but are not limited to:

  • Managing wound care and medical/diagnostic packs
  • Recording inventory of supplies and dispensing medications upon clinician requests
  • Checking the vitals signs of every patient who will be seen by a clinician and reporting them to the assigned CHW

This opportunity is a great fit for students interested in medicine, social services and mental health.

Mental Health Clinician

Duties include, but are not limited to:

  • Providing psychosocial assessment, including assessment for safety
  • Offering therapeutic intervention to stabilize persons experiencing trauma or crisis
  • Giving referrals to outpatient clinic or community resources

NOTE: Our patients and teams rely on consistent attendance. On average, any volunteer position requires about 4 hours of time each month depending on level of involvement. We ask that you consider this commitment fully before applying.

No medical or social work background?

We need your help with office duties, transportation and assistance in various sectors of our work.

Office Support

Duties include, but are not limited to:

  • Greeting patients
  • Alleviating any patient concerns
  • Assisting with hospitatlity items (water, socks, etc.)

In the case of the client requiring medical attention, the office support staff gathers the chief complaint, assigns a CHW to the encounter and triages the patients based on medical priority.
